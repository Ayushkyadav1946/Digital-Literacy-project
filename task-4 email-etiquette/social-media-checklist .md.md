#  Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Task 4 – Part B | CSE0001 Digital Literacy | VIT Bhopal University**

---



# , Best Practices (Do This),

1,Treat your profile like a first impression. Use a clear photo and write a bio that reflects who you are as a student and professional.,

2,"Use platforms like LinkedIn to your advantage. Share your coding projects, wins, and the new skills you are learning."

3,"Respect your friends' privacy. Always ask for a quick ""okay"" before tagging them or posting their photos."

4,"Religiously log out of your accounts when using library, lab, or shared computers."


5,"Audit your privacy settings regularly. App updates can sometimes quietly reset your preferences back to ""public."

# ,Red Flags (Don't Do This)

1 ,Don't post anything you’d scramble to delete if a professor or hiring manager saw it. The internet never forgets.

2,"Don't treat public platforms like a diary. Avoid complaining about college, professors, or personal drama online."

3,Don't accept requests from total strangers just to boost your follower count. Quality over quantity.

4,Don't leave yourself logged in on a public device. It’s an open invitation for someone to hijack your account.

5,"Don't blindly give apps access to your contacts, location, or camera unless it is a must need for it to function."
