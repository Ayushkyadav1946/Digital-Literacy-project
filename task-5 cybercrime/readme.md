
# 📘 Cyber Safety Awareness Project

### *Understanding UPI Fraud & Staying Safe Online*

---

## 👋 About This Project

This project is a simple yet powerful attempt to spread awareness about **cyber fraud and online safety**, especially among students in India.

It combines:

* A **realistic case study** showing how online payment fraud happens
* A **practical prevention checklist** that anyone can follow

The goal is not just to inform—but to make people pause, think, and stay safe online.

---

## 📖 What’s Inside

### 1. 💸 Case Study: *“The ₹50,000 That Vanished in 90 Seconds”*

This case study tells the story of **Arjun**, a college student who lost ₹50,000 in a matter of minutes while trying to sell his laptop online.

👉 

#### 🔍 What You’ll Learn:

* How scammers **target sellers on platforms like OLX**
* The trick behind **fake UPI QR codes**
* Why entering your **UPI PIN = sending money**
* The emotional and financial impact of fraud

#### ⚠️ Key Lesson:

> If you are asked to enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money, it is always a scam.

---

### 2. 🛡️ Prevention Checklist: *Stay Safe Online*

This checklist is designed especially for **college students** and everyday internet users.

👉 

#### ✅ Covers Important Topics Like:

* Creating **strong and unique passwords**
* Enabling **two-factor authentication (2FA)**
* Identifying **phishing emails and scams**
* Safe usage of **UPI and digital payments**
* Avoiding risks on **public Wi-Fi**
* Being careful with **unknown apps and permissions**
* Handling **cyberbullying and suspicious activity**

#### 🚨 Golden Rules:

* Never share **OTP, PIN, or CVV**
* Always verify before clicking anything
* Trust your instincts when something feels off

---

## 🆘 What To Do If Something Goes Wrong

If you ever become a victim of cyber fraud:

* 🌐 Report online: [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* 📞 Call helpline: **1930 (24x7)**

> ⏱️ The faster you report, the higher the chances of recovering your money.

## 🎯 Why This Matters

Cyber fraud is not just a “tech issue”—it’s a **real-life problem** affecting students, families, and professionals every day.

This project reminds us:

> You don’t need to be an expert to stay safe online.
> You just need to **pause and think before you act**.
