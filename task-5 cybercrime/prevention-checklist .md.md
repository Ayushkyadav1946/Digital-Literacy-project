## Your Personal Safety Checklist

---

### 1. Never use the Same Password for All Accounts

We all do this. Using one password for everything because it is easy to remember.

But here is the truth: if a hacker gets into one account they get into all of your accounts.

**What to do instead:**

- Use a different and strong password for every important account like your email, banking and social media accounts

- Make sure your password is at least 8 characters long. Having a mix of letters,numbers and symbols

> "Think of your password like your toothbrush. Do not share it and change it regularly."

---

### 2. Always Turn On Two-Factor Authentication

A password alone is not enough anymore to protect you from online scams.

Two-factor authentication adds a second lock to your account. Even if someone steals your password they still cannot get your information without your phone.

**Enable two-factor authentication:**

- Your college email

- Your Gmail or Google account

- Your Instagram, Facebook and Snapchat accounts

- Your WhatsApp account

- Any banking or UPI app

> "It takes 2 minutes to set up ,but it Could save you from a lifetime of regret."

---

### 3.Never Fall for "Your Account Has Been Suspended" type  Emails

This is phishing.. It works because it panics you into acting fast without thinking.

** flags to watch for:**

- Never panic after seeing Urgent language like "Act immediately" or "Your account will be deleted"

- Links that look almost right but are not

- Requests for your OTP, password or card number via email or SMS

**What to do:** Close the email. Open a tab. Go directly to your original bank website by typing the URL yourself.

> "Your bank never asks for your OTP or password over email, SMS or phone."

---

### 4. UPI Safety. You Enter Your PIN to Send Money Not to Receive

This is the important financial safety tip and the one most people get wrong.

**Here is the truth about UPI:**

- To receive money. You do nothing. No PIN is needed. No approval is required.

- To send money. You enter your MPIN.

**Common scam:** Someone calls saying "I am sending you 5,000 rupees. Just approve this request."

The moment you enter your MPIN on that request you are the one sending money. Not them.

> "If someone asks you to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. It is a scam. Every single time."

---

### 5. UPI Safety. Never Share Your OTP, MPIN or CVV With Anyone

It does not matter if the caller says they are from your bank from Google Pay support from NPCI or even from your college.

Any legitimate organisation will ever ask for your UPI MPIN, your ATM PIN, your OTP or your card CVV or expiry date.

**What to do if someone asks:** Hang up. Then call your bank directly using the number on the back of your card.

> "Sharing your OTP is like handing someone your house key and asking them to check something."

---

### 6. Be Careful while using Public Wi-Fi

College canteens, libraries, cafes, railway stations. Free Wi-Fi is everywhere. avoid it until necessary 

So are the people watching what you do on it.

**On Wi-Fi, never:**

- Log into your bank account or UPI app

- Enter passwords or personal details

- Shop online or make payments

**What to do instead:**

- Always use your mobile data for anything sensitive

> "using Public Wi-Fi is like having a conversation in a crowded room. People can listen."

---

### 7. Think Twice Before You Download any unknown App

Not every app on the Play Store or App Store is safe.

Some apps ask for permissions they never need to get access to your personal information without telling you.

**Before downloading ask:**

- Does this app really need access to my contacts? My camera? My location?

- Is it from a verified developer with reviews?

- Does it have a privacy policy?

**Beware of apps like :** Loan apps, exam cheat apps, free recharge apps and "earn money apps. Many are data harvesting tools or worse.

> "If a free app is asking for much you are probably the product. Not the customer."

---

### 8. Google Yourself Once in a While

You would be surprised what the internet knows about you. Social media posts, photos comments from years ago or even your phone number on some directory site.

**What to do:**

- Search your name on Google and see what it shows ,if it shows your photos or videos which you never posted online, in that case.

- Check your social media apps privacy settings, and restrict the permissions which are not required by the app.

- Remove or restrict anything you never want your future employer or professor to find

---

### 9. If Someone Online Makes You Uncomfortable. Trust That Feeling that something is wrong

Cyberbullying, online harassment and unsolicited messages are very common now a days but many people never admit that

You do not have to tolerate it. You do not have to handle it alone.

**What to do:**

- Screenshot the conversation as evidence

- Block. Report the account on the platform

- Talk to a friend whom you trust, family member or college counsellor about what happened with you.

- If it is serious or threatening file a complaint

## Something Went Wrong? Report It Immediately.

If you or someone in your known has become a victim of cybercrime. Do not wait do not feel embarrassed. Do not think that nothing can be done.

---

###  Link of National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal

> **(https://cybercrime.gov.in)**

- 24hours,7days a week

- Report financial fraud, phishing, UPI scams, cyberbullying, identity theft, sextortion and more

- You can report anonymously for some categories to protect your identity.

- It is  Managed by the **Ministry of Home Affairs Government of India**

---

### Cyber Crime Helpline Number: 1930

> Call 1930. Toll Free number, 24x7 available across India

- for financial cyber fraud. The faster you call the helpline, the higher are the chance of freezing the transaction and recovering your money.

- Available in languages like Hindi and English

- Operated by the **Indian Cybercrime Coordination Centre (I4C)**

> "Time matters in fraud. Call 1930 within the few hours of the incident for the best chance of recovery."
