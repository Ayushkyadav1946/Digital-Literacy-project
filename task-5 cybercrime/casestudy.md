Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

"The ₹50,000 That Vanished in 90 Seconds"

I want to tell you about Arjun. He is a 21-year- engineering student in Pune. Arjun was selling his laptop on OLX to get some money for his semester fees. He put up the listing on a Sunday evening. By Monday morning someone wanted to buy it.

This buyer seemed good. He was polite and did not try to bargain. He said "I will pay you now using Google Pay". He also said "just scan this QR code to confirm your account".

Arjun scanned the QR code. His phone asked him for his UPI PIN. He entered it because he thought it was just to verify his account.

In 90 seconds ₹50,000 was taken out of his account.

🔍 How It Happened. Step by Step

Step 1. The Setup: The person who did this fraud was looking for people who were selling things on OLX and Quikr. He wanted to find people who needed money and were waiting for someone to pay them.

Step 2. Building Trust: He called Arjun and spoke to him in a way. He agreed to the price away and made it seem like he had to do it fast. "I am travelling tomorrow so lets do this now".

Step 3. The Trick: He sent a request that looked like a payment QR code. When you enter your PIN for a request in UPI it means you are sending money not getting it.

Step 4. The Steal: soon as Arjun entered his MPIN ₹50,000 was sent to another account. One that could not be traced. This all happened in a few minutes.

Step 5. Gone: The person who did this fraud stopped answering his phone. The account he used on OLX was fake.

👥 Who Usually Gets Targeted by UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI/Online Payment Fraud usually targets students and young adults who are selling things like gadgets, books or furniture on websites where you can sell things

UPI/Online Payment Fraud also targets people who are selling things in their homes on websites like OLX, Quikr or Facebook Marketplace

Senior citizens who do not know how UPI collect requests work are also targeted by UPI/Online Payment Fraud

Small vendors and freelancers who are waiting for money from new clients can also be targeted by UPI/Online Payment Fraud

Army personnel and government employees are also targeted by UPI/Online Payment Fraud. Sometimes the person who does the fraud pretends to be one of them

⚠️ What Happens After UPI/Online Payment Fraud

What happened after Arjun was a victim of UPI/Online Payment Fraud was very bad:

He lost a lot of money. ₹50,000 Was gone. This was the money he needed for his semester fees and the money he got from selling his laptop. It was all gone.

He felt very bad emotionally. He felt ashamed blamed himself and was worried about using payments again

The bank could not help him get his money back right away. It can take weeks or months to get the money back and there is no guarantee

He lost trust in using UPI. Arjun stopped using UPI for months even when he needed to make real transactions

His phone number was also at risk. He got many fake calls for months after this happened

🚨 The Important Rule About UPI/Online Payment Fraud That Arjun Did Not Know

Arjun wishes he knew this rule. When you use UPI you enter your PIN to send money not to get it. If a QR code asks for your PIN it is taking money from you not giving it to you.

If something like this happens to you you should call 1930 away. The you report it the higher the chance of stopping the transaction before the money is sent further.
