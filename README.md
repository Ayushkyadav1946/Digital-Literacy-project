### 🔐 Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness: Case Study & Prevention Guide
**Module 5 | 20 Marks**
 
**Part A — Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud**
 
> *Meet Arjun — a 21-year-old engineering student in Pune selling his old laptop on OLX to cover semester fees. A buyer called within hours, sounded perfect, and asked him to "scan a QR code to confirm his account." Arjun scanned it, entered his UPI PIN — and ₹50,000 left his account in 90 seconds.*
 
The case study covers:
- What the crime is and how it works (step-by-step)
- Who is usually targeted (students, seniors, small vendors)
- The real consequences — financial, emotional, and long-term
 
**Part B — Stay Safe Online Checklist (10 Tips):**
 
The checklist includes specific UPI safety rules, phishing awareness, password hygiene, and app safety — written for college students in India.

## 💡 What I Actually Learned
 
I'll be honest — when I started this project, I thought it would be straightforward. Write some emails, make a pretty slide, submit.
 
It turned out to be a lot more than that.
 
- I learned that **phishing works not because people are foolish**, but because it's designed to exploit fear and urgency
- I learned that **entering your UPI PIN means you're sending money** — and that a lot of students don't know this
- I learned that **a badly written email can cost you an opportunity** just as much as a skill gap can
- I learned that **GitHub is not just for coders** — it's a portfolio, a resume, and a public record of your growth
- And I learned that **digital literacy is not a one-time lesson** — it's a habit you build one good decision at a time
